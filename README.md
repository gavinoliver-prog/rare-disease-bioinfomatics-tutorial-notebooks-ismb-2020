# Translational RNA-Seq Analysis for Rare Genetic Disease

This repository contains three Jupyter notebooks demonstrating practical bioinformatics workflows used in the **translational analysis of RNA-Seq data for rare genetic disease investigation**.

The notebooks illustrate three complementary analytical strategies frequently used in transcriptome-based rare disease diagnostics:

- **Fusion transcript detection**
- **Outlier gene expression analysis**
- **Outlier splicing analysis**

Each notebook is associated with a **real rare disease diagnostic case** in which transcriptomic analysis contributed to identifying the underlying genetic mechanism. The corresponding cases have been **peer-reviewed and published in the scientific literature**.

## Notebook Availability

The notebooks included in this repository contain the **original generated outputs from the analyses**, allowing them to be inspected and followed step-by-step.

However:

- The **original underlying datasets have been removed**
- As a result, the notebooks **cannot be executed from scratch**

They are therefore provided **for reference and educational purposes**, illustrating the analytical workflows and interpretation of results used in transcriptome-based rare disease investigation.

## Tutorial Context

RNA-Seq has emerged as a powerful complementary modality for investigating unresolved genetic disease. Transcriptomic data enable multiple analytical approaches capable of revealing disease mechanisms that may be missed by traditional DNA-based testing.

These approaches include:

- gene expression outlier detection  
- aberrant splicing identification  
- fusion transcript discovery  
- allele-specific expression analysis  

Recent studies have demonstrated that RNA-Seq-based analysis can significantly increase diagnostic yield in rare disease investigations.

The ISMB tutorial introduced these analytical paradigms and explored their translational application in clinical genomics. Participants were guided through practical workflows demonstrating how RNA-Seq data can be analyzed and interpreted to prioritize candidate disease mechanisms.

Hands-on sessions illustrated the use of specific computational tools and strategies for filtering, prioritizing, and interpreting transcriptomic signals in the context of patient phenotype and biological relevance.

## Purpose of This Repository

This repository preserves the tutorial notebooks as a **reference resource illustrating real analytical workflows used in transcriptome-based rare disease diagnostics**.

The examples demonstrate how multiple complementary RNA-Seq analyses can be integrated to support the identification of candidate pathogenic mechanisms and improve the likelihood of achieving a genetic diagnosis in rare disease patients.
